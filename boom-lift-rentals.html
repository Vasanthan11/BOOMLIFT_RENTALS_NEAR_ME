Boom lift types

Boom lift options available near your job site

Different boom lift types solve slightly different challenges. These quick descriptions help you understand where each type fits before you finalise your rental.

Articulated

Articulated boom lifts for working around obstacles

Articulated booms use multiple joints to move up and over ducts, canopies and structures. They are useful when the lift cannot be placed directly below the work area.

Suitable for façade work, signage, maintenance and areas with overhead interference.
Telescopic

Telescopic boom lifts for straight reach at height

Telescopic booms extend in a straight line to reach tall structures and long spans. They are preferred where maximum reach and quick positioning are important.

Common on industrial sites, infrastructure work and open areas with clear movement paths.
Electric & diesel

Power choices based on your working environment

Diesel booms are robust for outdoor and mixed-use projects, while electric or hybrid options are chosen for enclosed spaces where emissions and noise must be controlled.

We can guide you on which power source fits your site policy and layout.

Where boom lifts help

Typical jobs that regularly use boom lifts near you

Boom lifts are chosen wherever you need repeat access at height, outreach over obstacles and safer working positions than basic ladders or temporary assemblies.

Façade and glazing

Working on glass panels, cladding, external painting and branding on mid-rise and high-rise façades.

Industrial maintenance

Access to pipe racks, trusses, tanks and tall structures during planned shutdowns or ongoing maintenance.

Signage and outdoor media

Installation and replacement of signboards, hoardings and lighting structures facing roads and public areas.

Infrastructure and utilities

Working along bridges, flyovers, utility corridors and street lighting projects where reach is critical.

Services installation

Electrical cable trays, lighting, communication lines and other services routed at height across large facilities.

Cleaning and surface prep

Facade cleaning, pressure washing and preparation before recoating or repair work at height.
